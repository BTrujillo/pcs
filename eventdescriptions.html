Involving Our Community

There are many events hosted by Project Caring & Sharing! We host backpack give aways for the holidays, going back to school, along with many other programs and events.

Go to Calendar

Mentorship

Usually hosted on Tuesdays and Thursdays, the mentorship program allows for youths to work one on one with our student volunteers to improve their studying abilities and get a better understanding of their schoolwork.

For more information about the volunteering program and how to get involved, click the link below.

Mentorship Program

Summer and Winter Day Camps

The camps are held during the traditional summer school breaks. Each camp provides extensive education and social development activities for children entering the 6th, 7th, and 8th grades.

Back to School Backpack Giveaways

Before students return to school, Project: Caring & Sharing hosts a back-to-school backpack giveaway. Students receive supplies that will help them be successful in their studies and in their lives.

The Next Step Program

The next step program is a college admission test preparation and financial aid assistant program.

Mentoring Children of Incarcerated Parents Program

This is a structured one-to-one relationship between a young person, ages 8-17, who has one or both parents incarcerated and a loving, caring volunteer.
